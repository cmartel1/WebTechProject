Balance is hard...

3/24/23 Colby Martel

A year ago this week I took down every video on my channel and started fresh. I used to upload basketball compilations on camera and even now as much as I love showcasing other people through my interviews. A large part of why I do that is because I have difficulties expressing myself and want to listen to others instead. Even so, interviewing strangers has been way outside my comfort zone and what's happened in this past year has been well beyond my highest expectations. To begin last semester, I swore to myself that I would upload one long-form video every single Wednesday no matter what and last November, I even committed to posting daily short form content as well. Tthe results were incredible. But now, I've gone almost two months without a long-form post, and have uploaded very few Shorts. Assignments are stacking in each of my classes, I'm trying to organize weekly meetings for a Creator Club I started, serve as President for the largest club on campus, and prepare for my Art Showcase next Friday, taking night classes for content creation and 18 credits at an elite business school. I just want to be the best that I can be at everything that I do but it's impossible to put full-time hours and energy into everything. And now, I want to spend more time with friends and family and not put so much pressure on myself to meet my other expectations.

One of my favorite quotes is from Theodore Roosevelt when he says, "comparison is the thief of joy." I think I've done a great job at not comparing myself to others despite how easy that is to do nowadays but instead I've been comparing myself to myself and I think that's even less healthy. You might think that's good: you should compare yourself to yourself and try to be better every day, but I'm not comparing myself to who I was yesterday, I'm comparing myself to the ideal perfect version of myself that does not and will not ever exist. The guy who's always happy but very disciplined too, the guy who wakes up early and works on his business before he goes to class and then at class is actively engaged and fully prepared to participate and add to conversations, the guy who runs every day and never wastes time on his phone, who eats healthy and has healthy relationships with everyone that he meets, who reads before bed and gets eight hours of sleep every night. Although I'm doing a lot of those things, I'm not doing them to the quality that I'm hoping for. I just want to put all my energy into everything that I do but I'm stress myself out and just want everything to slow down. As I attempt to be the best at everything, I'm not reaching my potential in anything.

We've come so far this year and yet all I can think about is what's next rather than stopping to appreciate what's happened and embrace the present moment. This outlook though can be daunting and probably has contributed to how I'm feeling right now, but these habits and goals that I'm setting are not that difficult to achieve and are well within my control. What's been holding me back has been the strain of perfectionism. I don't just want to run four miles a day I want to do it under 30 minutes and I don't want to just upload every week I want to upload the best video possible every week and I just need to be reminded why I started this channel in the first place. It wasn't to stress myself out but because I love to make videos and share them with others. So as much as I keep pushing to be where I want to be, I now realize that we're already here as long as I'm uploading videos sharing them with others and enjoying the process I'm exactly where I want to be. Rather than wanting time to slow down, I just need to slow down myself and chill out a bit. I'm excited for what's next I'm even more grateful for what's now.
